## DTC (Direct to Consumer)

### State-by-State DTC Shipping

- DTC shipping regulations vary dramatically by state
- Some states allow unlimited DTC wine shipping; others prohibit it entirely
- Beer DTC is far more restricted than wine DTC in most states
- Spirits DTC is the most restricted category
- DTC shipping does not replace the distributor — it creates a parallel channel
  that primarily benefits small producers

### Impact on Three-Tier Distribution

- DTC is not replacing the three-tier system — franchise laws and state regulations
  ensure distributors remain the primary channel
- DTC primarily serves small, niche producers who cannot get distributor attention
- Large producers still route 95%+ of volume through distributors
- The bigger disruption is digital ordering within the three-tier system
  (retailers ordering from distributors via app instead of paper/phone)

## Consolidation

### Distributor Consolidation

- The number of beer distributors in the US has dropped from ~4,700 (2000) to ~3,000 (2025)
- Top 10 distributors now control ~40% of total volume
- Major consolidators: Reyes Holdings, Southern Glazer's, Breakthru Beverage, RNDC
- Smaller distributors survive through local market expertise and craft/specialty portfolios

### Impact on Technology

- Consolidation drives demand for multi-entity, multi-warehouse ERP systems
- Acquired distributors often run different ERPs (one on Encompass, another on VIP)
- Data integration across heterogeneous systems becomes critical post-acquisition
- Standardization on a common CRM layer (like Ohanafy/Salesforce) can bridge ERP differences
- Larger distributors can justify enterprise iPaaS investments (MuleSoft, Tray.io)

### Supplier Consolidation

- AB InBev, Molson Coors, and Constellation Brands dominate domestic production
- These mega-suppliers drive technology adoption through reporting requirements
- Distributors must report depletion data, promotional compliance, and survey results
  through supplier portals — each with different formats and requirements

## Technology Adoption

### Digital Ordering Platforms

- Retailers increasingly expect to order from distributors via web/mobile apps
- Platforms like Provi, LibDib, and SevenFifty are creating marketplace-style ordering
- Distributors must either integrate with these platforms or risk losing orders
- B2B e-commerce for beverage is growing 15-20% annually

### Inventory Management Automation

- Real-time inventory visibility is becoming table stakes
- Distributors are adopting warehouse management systems (WMS) with barcode/RFID scanning
- Automated pick-to-light and voice-directed picking in larger warehouses
- IoT temperature monitoring for cold chain compliance
- FIFO enforcement through system-directed picking (not driver choice)

### Data-Driven Territory Optimization

- Route optimization software adoption is accelerating (Roadnet/Descartes, Paragon)
- Moving from fixed routes to dynamic routing based on daily demand
- GPS tracking and geofenced check-ins for delivery verification
- Predictive analytics for demand forecasting (weather, events, seasonality)
- Goal tracking and territory performance dashboards replacing monthly paper reports

### Mobile-First Field Operations

- Every major system now requires a mobile-first field experience
- Offline capability is critical — rural delivery areas have unreliable cell service
- Camera integration for proof of delivery, shelf audits, display compliance
- GPS-aware for route tracking, mileage, and DOT compliance

### Integration-First Architecture

The industry is shifting from isolated systems to connected ecosystems:
- Cloud-native platforms (Salesforce, UKG) expose clean APIs
- iPaaS adoption (Tray.io, MuleSoft, Boomi) replacing fragile point-to-point integrations
- Moving from nightly batch updates to near-real-time data flows
- Unified data models that connect employee (HCM) + account (CRM) + product (ERP) + route

## Emerging Trends

### AI and Automation

- AI-powered demand forecasting using weather, events, and historical patterns
- Automated order suggestions based on account purchase history
- Chatbot-driven customer service for order status and invoice queries
- Predictive maintenance for delivery fleet

### Sustainability

- Returnable packaging programs (kegs, reusable crates) require reverse logistics tracking
- Carbon footprint tracking per delivery route
- Electric vehicle adoption for urban delivery routes
- Waste reduction through better date code management and demand forecasting
